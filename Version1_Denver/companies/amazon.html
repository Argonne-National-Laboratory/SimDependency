Amazon Web Services

Amazon Web Services, Inc. provides information technology infrastructure services to businesses in the form of Web services. Its products and solutions include cloud computing, compute, networking, storage and content delivery, databases, analytics, application services, deployment and management, mobile services, applications, AWS marketplace software, start-ups, enterprises, partners, government and education, Websites and Web applications, big data and HPC, gaming, and digital media; and backup, storage, and archive. The company also provides AWS management console, documentation, SDKs and tools, downloads, platforms, Java, JavaScript, mobile, PHP, Ruby, Python, Windows and .NET, resources, forums, tutorials and articles, whitepapers, training and certification, and AWS user groups for developers. In addition, it offers compute, storage, database, analytics, application, and deployment services; mobile services, Websites, and backup and recovery services; and Amazon WorkMail, a managed business email and calendaring service in the cloud. It serves government, education, and nonprofit sectors in North America, South America, Europe/the Middle East/Africa, the Asia Pacific, and internationally. The company was founded in 2006 and is based in Seattle, Washington. It has data center locations in the United States, Europe, Brazil, Singapore, Japan, and Australia. The company also has a brick-and-mortar retail bookstore in north Seattle. Amazon Web Services, Inc. operates as a subsidiary of Amazon.com Inc.
